Global Warming


Alarming Rise In Global Temperatures!



How has global average temperature changed over time as a result of air, land and water pollution?


From the shown line chart, it is quite clear that the average temperature is on rise since 1880. According to NOAA's 2020 Annual Climate Report the combined land and ocean temperature has increased at an average rate of 0.13 degrees Fahrenheit ( 0.08 degrees Celsius) per decade since 1880; however, the average rate of increase since 1981 (0.18°C / 0.32°F) has been more than twice that rate.



Global Warming: Monthly Temperature Anomaly (Celsius)

CO2 Emissions Per Capita (tons)


Global climate change is mostly caused by carbon dioxide emissions. It is commonly acknowledged that, in order to avert the worst effects of climate change, the world must cut emissions as soon as possible. However, how this duty is divided across regions, governments, and people has long been a source of debate in international forums.

From the map it is evident that countries amongst most CO2 emissions per capita are Australia, Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Saudia Arabia and Canadia. What is also evident that per capita CO2 emitted from Mongolia is 6 times more than CO2 emitted from India and Pakistan combined.

CO2 Emissions Around The Globe


Pollution levels are at an all time-high!



How have global emissions of carbon dioxide (CO2) changed over time?


From the shown line chart, it is quite clear that the Co2 emissions have been on a steady rise. The world has seen a very short reduction is Co2 emissions few times since 1970, but there has not been a single reduction in nearly 12 years!! The reason of this dramatic increase is simply because of the fossil fuels that are being burnt around the world for energy.



Global Fossil Fuel Consumption






136761 TWh of fossil fuels were burned in 2019 for energy

Around the time of the Industrial Revolution, people began burning fossil fuels for energy. However, our use of fossil fuels has evolved dramatically throughout the ages, both in terms of what we burn and how much we burn.

In this graph, we see the global coal, oil and gas consumption. It is evident that from mid 20th century, fossil fuel consumption has been sky rocketed. Amongst the three fossil fuel types, oil seems to be the highest consumed.


Global primary energy consumption by fossil fuel source, measured in terawatt-hours (TWh)

Greenhouse Gas Emission






Food production accounts for one-quarter of the world’s greenhouse gas emissions and takes up half of the planet’s habitable surface.

The United Nations defines food, energy, and water as the "nexus" of sustainable development. As the world's population has expanded and become wealthier, demand for all three has risen fast. Not only has demand for all three increased, but they are also intricately linked: food production requires both water and energy, traditional energy production requires water resources, and agriculture offers a potential energy source.


Greenhouse gas emissions per kilogram of food product

Plastic Waste






Plastic sticks around in the environment for ages, threatening wildlife and spreading toxins. Plastic also contributes to global warming.

The graph depicts the rate of plastic trash generation per person as a percentage of gross domestic product (GDP) per capita. Plastic trash output tends to rise as we become wealthier, however there is substantial variance among nations at all levels of development. Plastic trash per capita is significantly lower in low-income areas.
