Pollution Around the Globe

To be more energy efficient, reducing the amount of energy we consume is an essential step. Consuming less oil decreases the need for our energy supplies, such as fossil fuels, which is critical for a safer and brighter future.

This visualisation is based on the dataset from "Our World In Data"




Pollution Emissions Per Capita (tons)


Here, we see the energy use per person in 2019. This is inclusive of all types of energy (electricity plus transport and heating). An interesting point to note is that global average per capita energy consumption has been on a consistent rise; average consumption has increased by approximately 45%.

What is also evident from the data shown is that the average US citizen consumes more than 10 times the energy of the average Indian, 4-5 times that of a Brazilian, and three times more than China. And, the gulf between these and very low-income nations is even greater!

Global Fossil Fuel Consumption

3400% Increase for China in investment

It would take substantial financial investment to move our energy structures away from fossil fuels toward sustainable energies. But how much do we actually spend in this sector, and how do we spread this funding around the world?

In this graph, we see global investments in renewable technologies from 2004 to 2015 (in billion USD per year). The world invested 47 billion USD in 2004. By 2015, this had grown to USD 286 billion, a growth of over 600 percent. China's rise was the fastest, growing from USD 3 billion in 2004 to USD 103 billion by 2015 (an increase of 3400 %). China is also the biggest single clean energy donor, spending about the same as the United States, Europe and India combined.
